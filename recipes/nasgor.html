Nasi Goreng

Description

Nasi goreng is an Indonesian fried rice dish that gained prominence internationally. It is served everywhere in Indonesia, from humble street vendors, to five-star hotels and upscale restaurants, with uncountable number of variations and recipes, with some influence from Chinese cuisine and the locality cuisine. In home cooking usually (as with fried rice in other countries) cooked with leftover food and dishes from previous day.

Ingredients

  • 2 plates of cold rice
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 5 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 3 red chilies (or to taste), thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (optional)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil for sautéing
  • 1 stalk scallion, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the garlic, shallots, and chili until fragrant.
  2. Push the aromatics to the side of the pan, pour in the beaten eggs, and scramble until cooked.
  3. Add the white rice and mix well with the aromatics and eggs.
  4. Add sweet soy sauce, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir until all the seasonings are evenly distributed.
  5. Add the scallions and stir briefly until they wilt.
  6. Remove from heat and serve with crackers, pickles, and side dishes of your choice.
