Sate

Description

Sate is a dish made from meat cut into small pieces and skewered—typically using the midrib of a coconut leaf or a bamboo stick—then grilled over wood charcoal embers.

Ingredients

  • 500 g beef tenderloin
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Satay skewers

Spice Paste:

  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1/2 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 2 tbsp fried shallots
  • 1 tsp granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp salt

Peanut Sauce:

  • 250 ml water
  • 2 limes
  • 10 g palm sugar
  • 50 g fried peanuts
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 curly red chilies
  • 1 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Slice the beef against the grain, then cut it into 2x2 cm cubes.
  2. Thoroughly mix the beef cubes with the spice paste and oil. Let marinate for 30 minutes.
  3. Thread 3–4 pieces of beef onto each skewer.
  4. Grill the skewers over hot coals, turning them frequently until cooked through on both sides, then remove from the heat.
  5. Peanut Sauce: Combine the spice paste with water, cook briefly until slightly thickened, then remove from the heat.
  6. Stir in the lime juice and let the sauce cool.
  7. Serve the warm beef satay with the peanut sauce.
