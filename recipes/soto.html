Soto

Description

Soto (also known by various local names such as sroto, sauto, tauto, or coto) is a traditional Indonesian dish resembling soup, made from meat broth and vegetables. Beef and chicken are the most commonly used meats, though pork, horse, or goat meat are also sometimes used.

Ingerdients

  • 1/2 broiler chicken, cut into 4 pieces
  • 1,750 ml water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 5 lime leaves
  • 1 cm galangal, bruised
  • 2 cm ginger, bruised
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons oil for sautéing

Spice paste:

  • 8 shallots
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 cm turmeric, roasted

Bird's eye chili sambal:

  • 15 bird's eye chilies
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Accompaniments:

  • 150 g cabbage, sliced
  • 100 g bean sprouts, blanched
  • 2 stalks celery, finely sliced
  • 3 limes
  • 25 g fried shallots
  • 1,000 g steamed white rice

Steps

  1. In a pot, boil the chicken, bay leaves, lime leaves, galangal, and ginger. Add salt and granulated sugar.
  2. Sauté the spice paste until fragrant, then add it to the boiling broth in the pot. Cook the broth until the chicken is fully cooked.
  3. While waiting for the soto broth to cook, prepare the soto chili sauce by first boiling the chilies and garlic. Mash them into a paste and add salt. Set aside.
  4. Arrange the soto ingredients in a bowl, pour the hot broth over them, and enjoy with the chili sauce and other accompaniments.
